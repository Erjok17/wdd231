How to Create a Winning Basketball Recruiting Video

Creating a standout recruiting video is crucial to catching the attention of coaches and scouts. Follow these steps to ensure your video highlights your skills effectively:

1. Gather Game Footage

  • Use only game footage—no practice or drills.
  • Include 30-40 plays that showcase your best moments.
  • Ensure the competition level is strong to demonstrate your ability against skilled opponents.

2. Set Up the Camera Properly

  • Use a tripod to keep the footage stable and professional.
  • Position the camera to capture the full mid-court view—avoid obstructed angles or crowd interference.
  • Do not zoom in and out; keep the frame wide enough to show the entire play.

3. Highlight Key Skills

Coaches want to see a well-rounded player. Include plays that demonstrate:

  • Shooting capability: Show a variety of shots (3-pointers, mid-range, free throws).
  • Scoring ability: Highlight drives to the basket and finishing through contact.
  • Footwork and quickness: Display agility, speed, and defensive movement.
  • Finishing at the rim: Include layups, dunks, and contested finishes.
  • All-around athleticism: Showcase rebounding, fast breaks, and hustle plays.
  • Ball handling: Demonstrate dribbling skills, crossovers, and breaking presses.
  • Blocking shots: Include defensive plays like blocks and steals.

4. Keep It Clean and Professional

  • Avoid special effects, music, or flashy transitions. Let your skills speak for themselves.
  • Start the video with a brief introduction: State your name, position, height, weight, and graduation year.
  • End with your contact information (email, phone number, and social media handles).

5. Edit and Organize

  • Arrange plays in a logical order (e.g., scoring, defense, athleticism).
  • Keep the video 3-5 minutes long—coaches prefer concise, impactful footage.
  • Use simple text overlays to label plays if necessary (e.g., "3-Pointer," "Fast Break Dunk").

6. Share Your Video

  • Upload the video to platforms like YouTube or Vimeo for easy sharing.
  • Share the link with coaches, recruiters, and on your social media profiles.
  • Include the link in your player profile on recruiting websites.

By following these guidelines, you’ll create a recruiting video that highlights your skills, impresses coaches, and opens doors to scholarships and opportunities. Your talent deserves to be seen—make it happen!
